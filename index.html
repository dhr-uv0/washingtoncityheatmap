Key Metrics

Methodology: 5 weighted criteria normalized 0–100 across the WA city dataset. Adjust weights via the ⚖️ Scoring Weights button. Sources: U.S. Census Bureau ACS (2023), County Business Patterns CBP (2022), BLS Business Employment Dynamics.

Data Sources & Methodology

All data sourced from U.S. federal public APIs. Some city-level values are estimated from county-level data apportioned by city employment share — these cells are marked estimated and should be verified before investment decisions.

MetricSourceUpdate Frequency
SMB Definition (Industry Standard for Exit / M&A Advisory)
Employees 5–249 · Estimated Revenue $500K–$50M · Target Sectors: manufacturing, wholesale, retail, food service, healthcare, professional services, construction, logistics (NAICS 23, 31–33, 42, 44–45, 48–49, 54, 62, 72).
Tier Classification System
Cities are classified into 5 tiers by population size (ACS 2023). Note: Tier 3 = prime target zone (mid-markets 30k–59k population). Tier 1 = major hubs (100k+) — oversaturated with national consulting firms. Tier 3 mid-markets are large enough for consistent SMB pipeline, small enough to be underserved by national competitors.

Tier Guide: Tier 1 (100k+) · Tier 2 (60k–99k) · Tier 3 (30k–59k) · Tier 4 (15k–29k) · Tier 5 (<15k)
Consulting Firm Count Methodology
Counts all NAICS 5416 (Management, Scientific, and Technical Consulting Services) establishments per county from CBP 2022, apportioned to cities by population share with urban density adjustment. Approximately 20% of NAICS 5416 firms offer M&A or exit-advisory services specifically.
How to Use This Tool
1. Explore the map — taller and redder columns = higher opportunity
2. Click any city column or ranking to view full analysis
3. Use the ⚖️ Scoring Weights button to adjust criteria emphasis
4. Filter by tier using the sidebar buttons
5. Export data via ⬇ Export CSV for further analysis
