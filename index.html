Tier Classification System
Cities are classified into 5 tiers by population size (ACS 2023). Note: Tier 3 = prime target zone (mid-markets 30k–59k population). Tier 1 = major hubs (100k+) — oversaturated with national consulting firms. Tier 3 mid-markets are large enough for consistent SMB pipeline, small enough to be underserved by national competitors.

Tier Guide: Tier 1 (100k+) · Tier 2 (60k–99k) · Tier 3 (30k–59k) · Tier 4 (15k–29k) · Tier 5 (<15k)
Consulting Firm Count Methodology
Counts all NAICS 5416 (Management, Scientific, and Technical Consulting Services) establishments per county from CBP 2022, apportioned to cities by population share with urban density adjustment. Approximately 20% of NAICS 5416 firms offer M&A or exit-advisory services specifically.

What Are the Rankings Based On?

Each city receives a 0–100 Opportunity Score built from five weighted criteria. The score identifies underserved markets where a consulting or advisory practice can enter with minimal competition and a strong pipeline of potential clients.

CriterionWhat It MeasuresDefault Weight
🏢 Low Competition Fewer consulting firms in the city = higher score. Avoids oversaturated markets with compressed margins and high client acquisition cost. 30%
🏬 SMB Density Target-sector small businesses (5–249 employees) per 1,000 residents. Higher density = larger addressable pipeline for advisory mandates. 25%
🏙️ City Size & Profile Mid-sized cities (Tier 3, 30k–59k pop) score highest — large enough for quality engagements, small enough to be underserved by national firms. 20%
📅 Business Maturity % of businesses older than 3 years. Established owners have survived startup risk and are actively considering exit or succession timelines. 15%
👴 Succession Urgency % of population aged 55+, used as a proxy for Baby Boomer owner retirement readiness. Higher % = more owners entering their exit-planning window now. 10%

How Do I Adjust the Scoring Weights?

Click the ⚖️ Scoring Weights button in the header to open the weights panel. Each criterion has a slider and +/− buttons. The tool automatically rebalances the other sliders so the total always stays at 100%. When you're done adjusting, click Recalculate to re-score and re-rank all 100 cities instantly. Click Reset to Defaults to restore the original 30/25/20/15/10 split. Weights are not saved between sessions.

Example: If you're entering a market primarily to avoid competition rather than maximize pipeline, drag "Low Competition" up to 50%+ — cities like Ellensburg and Walla Walla will jump in rank.

How Are the Tiers Defined?

Cities are grouped into 5 tiers by population size. Tier 3 is the prime target zone — big enough to generate consistent advisory work, small enough that national consulting firms haven't saturated it.

TierPopulationLabelStrategy Note
Tier 1 100,000+ Saturated Market Seattle, Spokane, Bellevue — national firms dominate every niche. Entry cost is high; avoid unless you have a protected referral channel.
Tier 2 60k–99k High Competition Secondary metros with organized competition. Differentiation required — a specialty niche or anchor relationship is essential.
Tier 3 ★ 30k–59k Prime Opportunity The target zone. Enough SMB pipeline for a full advisory practice; underserved by national competitors. Enter this cycle.
Tier 4 15k–29k Emerging Market Real opportunity but territory coverage needs to span the broader county or sub-region to hit practice-sustaining deal cadence.
Tier 5 <15k Micro Market Insufficient standalone pipeline. Viable only as an ancillary stop within a multi-city territory anchored by a higher-tier primary.
