Key Metrics
Scoring methodology: Composite of 5 weighted criteria (consulting absence 30%, business abundance 25%, city tier 20%, maturity 15%, owner age 10%). All sub-scores normalized 0–100 relative to Washington State city dataset. Adjust weights via the ⚖️ button above.

📋 Data Sources & Methodology

All data is sourced from U.S. federal public APIs or the Washington Secretary of State business registry. Where city-level data was unavailable, county-level data was apportioned by city employment/population share. Data marked estimated in the city detail panel should be refreshed on the schedules below before making investment decisions.

SMB Definition (Industry Standard for Exit / M&A Advisory)
Employee range: 5–249 (excludes micro solo-owner businesses lacking M&A exit value; upper bound targets pre-mid-market segment). Revenue proxy: ~$500K–$50M. Sectors: manufacturing, wholesale, retail, food service, healthcare, professional services, construction, logistics (NAICS 23, 31-33, 42, 44-45, 48-49, 62, 72).
City Tier Classification (WA-Scale Adaptation)
Tiers derived from equal weighting of population size, median household income, and population growth 2010–2020 (all normalized 0–100 within WA dataset). Composite score → categorical tier assignment. Tier 3 (optimal target zone) is calibrated to 30k–95k population markets with established economic diversity — large enough for consistent SMB pipeline, small enough to be underserved by national and regional consulting competitors.
